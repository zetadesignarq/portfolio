About

This is Emily Zavala…

An Ecuadorian architect with an international background that has shaped the way I design through curiosity, exploration, and continuous learning.

I studied Architecture at Tecnológico de Monterrey in Monterrey, Mexico, a place that became my home for four years and where I had the opportunity to engage with different cultures, perspectives, and ways of understanding design. During that time, I discovered the richness of materials, proportion, and style, as well as the influence of renowned Latin American architecture masters.

Near the end of my degree, I traveled to Italy to earn a certification in Interior Design in Contemporary Living, completing a semester at the Istituto Lorenzo de’ Medici. That experience allowed me to dive deeper into contemporary interior design from a more artistic and detail-driven perspective, and it confirmed something that truly defines me: I love stepping outside my comfort zone to keep learning.

After completing one of the most important personal milestones of my life, earning my architecture degree, I knew it was essential to continue growing. I am currently pursuing my Master of Architecture at SCAD, where I’m expanding my vision, meeting new creative minds, and embracing the practices, concepts, and architectural systems unique to the United States. This stage excites me because it allows me to connect the best of Latin American construction knowledge with North American methods and standards.

I’m passionate about what is new, technological, artistic, and functional. I enjoy creating spaces that are not only visually compelling, even unconventional, but also purposeful and tailored to real life: daily routines, needs, and aspirations. For me, the essence of design happens when form and function come together with clarity and intention.

I’m ready to create eclectic spaces with character, bringing together the best of style, form, function, and identity to tell a story that feels uniquely yours.
